Introduction

  • Understanding privacy in New Zealand’s data environment is critical for research.
  • Māori data sovereignty ensures that Māori data is managed according to tikanga and principles of Te Tiriti o Waitangi.
  • Objectives for this session:
    • Review key privacy laws.
    • Introduce Māori data sovereignty concepts.
    • Discuss implications for social data research.

Privacy Laws in Aotearoa

  • Privacy Act 2020: Governs collection, storage, use, and sharing of personal data.
  • Key principles:
    • Purpose limitation
    • Data minimization
    • Transparency and accountability
    • Individual rights to access and correction
  • Enforcement through the Office of the Privacy Commissioner.

Māori Data Sovereignty

  • Definition: The right of Māori to control the collection, access, use, and sharing of data about Māori people, communities, and resources.
  • Core principles (Te Mana Raraunga):
    • Self-determination (tino rangatiratanga)
    • Collective benefit
    • Whakapapa (contextual understanding of data)
    • Accountability and governance
  • Implications:
    • Research must involve Māori governance.
    • Data sharing and usage require consent and alignment with tikanga.
